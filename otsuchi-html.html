Response to the Forest Fire in Otsuchi Town

Date: May 24, 2026

We would like to inform you about the response to the forest fire that occurred on April 22nd.

At 4:00 PM on May 12th, the Otsuchi Town Disaster Countermeasures Headquarters was transferred to the Otsuchi Town Disaster Alert Headquarters.

Traffic information, lifeline information, and disaster prevention information for Iwate Prefecture can be found on the Iwate Disaster Prevention Information Portal:

https://iwate-bousai.my.salesforce-sites.com/

Fire Incident Details

Two major forest fire locations were identified in Otsuchi Town:

  • 1st Area (Kozuchi): 26th Ward, Kozuchi, Otsuchi Town
  • 2nd Area (Kirikiri): 30th Ward, Otsuchi Town

The fires were first identified on April 22, 2020 at 13:53 and 16:28 respectively. The cause of the fire remains under investigation.

Burned Area Progression

The burned area expanded over multiple days:

  • April 24: Approximately 730 hectares total
  • April 25: Approximately 1,281 hectares total
  • April 26: Approximately 1,373 hectares total
  • April 27: Approximately 1,618 hectares total
  • April 28: Approximately 1,633 hectares total
  • April 29: Weather conditions prevented aerial confirmation
  • April 30: Approximately 1,633 hectares total
  • May 1 & 2: Weather conditions prevented aerial confirmation
  • May 3: No further increase observed, remaining at approximately 1,633 hectares

The fire in the Kozuchi area was officially suppressed on Saturday, May 2, 2020 at 13:00.

Additional Forest Fire Incident

Another forest fire occurred at 1:25 PM on Wednesday, May 6th, 2026, in the forest northwest of the Shiroyama Park Gymnasium in the 32nd district of Kozuchi, Otsuchi Town.

The fire was suppressed at 9:00 AM on Thursday, May 7th.

  • Burned area: Approximately 2 hectares
  • Casualties: None reported
  • Property damage: Under investigation
Note: As no changes were observed in the burned area after May 3rd, further burned area updates were discontinued.
